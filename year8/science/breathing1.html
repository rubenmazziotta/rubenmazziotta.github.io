How fish and insects breathe by Ruben Mazziotta

How fish breathe

Fish have gills and the way they works is the gills open when the fish needs air and when the gills open the water is passed through the gills. Next cells in the gills absorb the oxygen that is in the water; then blood passes the oxygen around the fish's body and then is returned as carbon dioxide which is released into the water.

How insects breathe

Breathing for insects takes place through the tracheae. Tracheae are tube structures that open on the insect's body through little holes called spiracles and each trachea has 4 tiny tubes which are named tracheoles. Air goes into the spiracles and all around the body of the insect and gives oxygen to the cells.
