WOOL

What is wool?

Wool is a textile that is obtained from sheeps and also from Cashmere goats, mohair, angora, rabbits and other animals. Here is a picture of a sheep:

What is wool used for?

Wool is used for different things example: making jumpers, scarves, coats. The annoying thing about wool is that it can sometimes make you itch!

How does wool grow on sheep?

Wool is grown on sheep exactly how humans and other animals grow hair. The difffernce is that we use that wool that is produced from sheep.

Wool manufacturing

To wear wool it has to be sheared off sheep and knitted to make it's form of scarve or something else. This is most the process of how wool is shown when you use it.

Shearing the sheep

1: The sheep has it's wool sheared off but that only happens once a year but still a shepard can shear about 200 sheep per day!

Grading and sorting

2: After shearing a sheep the next thing that happens is grading and sorting. Grading is when the fleece gets broken up by the overall quality. when sorting the wool it is broken up into different sections of different quality fibers from the different part of the sheeps body.

Cleaning the wool

3: When wool is sheared off a sheep it is also known as "raw" or "grease wool" which means that the wool is dirty (has sand, dirt,grease and dried sweat) and needs to be cleaned.

Carding wool

4: Wool is passed through alot of metal teeth that straightens the wool and blends it into slivers.

Spinning

5: Wool is made into thread by spinning the fibers together and that makes one strand of yarn.

Weaving

6: After spinning the wool into yarn it is woven into fabric. There are two fabrics one is loose and soft and one is tight and smooth.

Finishing

After doing everything from shearing the sheep to making the wool into fabric it is washed which makes it smaller then it is ready to be used.
